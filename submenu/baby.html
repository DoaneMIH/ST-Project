The first drug reference book in England was created in 1618.
The book, Pharmacopoeia londinensis, was backed by King James I and allowed the Royal College of Physicians to create a master list of all known medications, their indications for use, and their effects. Only drugs that appeared on the list were about to be produced or sold.


The first licensed pharmacist set up shop in the French Quarter.
Louis Dufilho Jr. of New Orleans became Americans first licensed pharmacist in the early 1800s. Prior to then, you did not need a license to become a pharmacist.

Procedures That Diagnose
Many procedures are presented to treat, but some are presented to screen a patient for a diagnosis. Here are some examples:


~ Sphygmomanometers (blood pressure kits) test blood pressure to screen for hypertension, cardiovascular disease, and diabetes.


~ Stethoscopes are controlled to listen to heart sounds to ensure the heartbeat is restored in strength and rhythm, monitor the lungs during inhalation and exhalation of breath, and blood flow in the artery veins.


~ Electrocardiographs include the electrical activity of the heart to screen for several forms of heart disease.


~ Thermometers, used at home and in healthcare treatment facilities, manage body temperature to screen for different health conditions.
